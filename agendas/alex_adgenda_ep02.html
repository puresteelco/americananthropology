CO-HOST CHEAT SHEET

AMERICAN ANTHROPOLOGY

The Turning Point (Nikolai Shmelev & Vladimir Popov) — Inside the Collapse: An Autopsy of Soviet Central Planning
1. Historical Context Ch. 1
Lenin’s NEP vs. Stalinist command planning; how market mixed systems saved the early USSR, while forced collectivization doomed it.
2. The Statistics Mirage Ch. 2
Fabricated Soviet growth data and Shmelev's famous line: "The ruble is no longer a currency, it is a lottery ticket."
3. The Administered System Ch. 3
The administrative command structure (Gosplan/Gossnab); the computational impossibility of fixing millions of prices.
4. Anatomy of a Deficit Ch. 4
The "Shortage Economy" and the Cheshire Cat analogy of Soviet supply and demand.
5. Black Holes of Waste Ch. 5
Overproducing raw inputs (steel/cement/tractors) while grain rots in the fields; meeting quotas by breaking machinery.
6. Prices & Incentives Ch. 6
The Moleskin Pelt example; why state-directed price setting fails and how we need market-driven incentives.
7. What to Sell Abroad Ch. 7
Autarky vs. integration; the convertibility barrier of the ruble and raw oil dependency.
8. The Turning Point Ch. 8
The final warning of Shmelev and Popov: either reform or become the global example of "how not to organize economic life."
"If we do not reform, we will turn into a backward, stagnant state that will be an example to the entire world of how not to organize economic life." — Shmelev & Popov
