# 🎙️ American Anthropology — Ep. 2: Shmelev & Popov's "The Turning Point"
**Co-Host Cheat Sheet** | **Lens:** Inside the Collapse: An Autopsy of Soviet Central Planning

Hey man! Here is the quick discussion guide for Episode 2. We are using the book's 8 chapters as our conversation rails to break down the collapse of the Soviet economy from the perspective of two reformist Soviet economists in 1989:

## 🕒 The 8-Part Discussion Guide (Topics Only)
1. **Historical Context (Ch. 1)**: Lenin’s NEP vs. Stalinist command planning; how market mixed systems saved the early USSR, while forced collectivization doomed it.
2. **The Statistics Mirage (Ch. 2)**: Fabricated Soviet growth data and Shmelev's famous line: "The ruble is no longer a currency, it is a lottery ticket."
3. **The Administered System (Ch. 3)**: The administrative command structure (Gosplan/Gossnab); the computational impossibility of fixing millions of prices.
4. **Anatomy of a Deficit (Ch. 4)**: The "Shortage Economy" and the Cheshire Cat analogy of Soviet supply and demand.
5. **Black Holes of Waste (Ch. 5)**: Overproducing raw inputs (steel/cement/tractors) while grain rots in the fields; meeting quotas by breaking machinery.
6. **Prices & Incentives (Ch. 6)**: The Moleskin Pelt example; why state-directed price setting fails and how we need market-driven incentives.
7. **What to Sell Abroad (Ch. 7)**: Autarky vs. integration; the convertibility barrier of the ruble and raw oil dependency.
8. **The Turning Point (Ch. 8)**: The final warning of Shmelev and Popov: either reform or become the global example of "how not to organize economic life."

---
> **💡 Key Quote:** *"If we do not reform, we will turn into a backward, stagnant state that will be an example to the entire world of how not to organize economic life."* — Nikolai Shmelev & Vladimir Popov

Let's read chapters 1-8 and prep! See you at the microphones.
