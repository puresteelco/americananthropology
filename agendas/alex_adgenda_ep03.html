CO-HOST CHEAT SHEET

AMERICAN ANTHROPOLOGY

The Rise of the West (William H. McNeill) — Friction of Strangers: Cultural Diffusion and the Eurasian Ecumene
1. The Sumerian Breakthrough Ch. 1 & 2
The move from self-sufficient neolithic villages to complex city-states; how Mesopotamian silt and irrigation forced the creation of central temple bureaucracies.
2. Egypt vs. Sumer Diffusion Ch. 3
Egypt's geographic isolation (autarky and stable pharaohs) vs. Sumer's open plain (fragmentation, dynamic trade, constant warfare).
3. The Cosmopolitan Chariot System Ch. 4
Chariot invasions integrating the Middle East; the bronze-to-iron shift; phonetic alphabet coordinating logistical empires.
4. Hellenic Freedom Ch. 5
The Greek city-state (*polis*); citizenship and rationalism emerging on the periphery, challenging Persia's bureaucratic empire.
5. The Eurasian Ecumene Ch. 6
The closure of the Silk Road chain (Rome, Parthia, Kush, Han China); how globalization connected Eurasian history into a single, vulnerable system.
"Historical change is primarily a matter of the contact of peoples, and the subsequent diffusion of techniques, ideas, and institutions... The engine of history is the friction of strangers." — McNeill
