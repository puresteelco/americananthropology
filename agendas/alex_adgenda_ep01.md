# 🎙️ American Anthropology — Ep. 1: Thomas Sowell's "Basic Economics"
**Co-Host Cheat Sheet** | **Lens:** Free Markets vs. The Failure of Socialist Central Planning

Hey man! Here is the quick discussion guide for our premiere episode. We are using the book's exact 7 parts as our conversation rails:

## 🕒 The 7-Part Discussion Guide (Topics Only)
1. **Prices & Markets (Ch. 1-6)**: Prices coordinate scarcity; contrast free price feedback loops with the structural shortages of socialist price controls and central planning.
2. **Industry & Commerce (Ch. 7-10)**: The self-correcting discipline of profits and losses; contrast market accountability with the systemic waste of subsidized state-run industries under socialism.
3. **Work & Pay (Ch. 11-14)**: How productivity determines real wages, contrasting voluntary labor value with state-directed wage fixing and centralized quota systems.
4. **Time & Risk (Ch. 15-18)**: The role of capital investment, risk-sharing, and how speculation coordinates future resource needs without a state planner.
5. **The National Economy (Ch. 19-21)**: Real wealth (physical output) vs. monetary tokens; why statist monetary expansion and deficit printing inevitably destroy currencies.
6. **The International Economy (Ch. 22-24)**: The non-zero-sum nature of voluntary trade and comparative advantage vs. state-directed protectionism.
7. **Special Issues (Ch. 25-27)**: Non-market decision-making; contrast political bureaucracy and NGO incentives with the direct accountability of free enterprise.

---
> **💡 Key Quote:** *"The first rule of economics is scarcity; the first rule of politics is to ignore the first rule of economics."* — Thomas Sowell
