CO-HOST CHEAT SHEET

AMERICAN ANTHROPOLOGY

Basic Economics (Thomas Sowell) — Free Markets vs. The Failure of Socialist Central Planning
1. Prices & Markets Ch. 1-6
Prices coordinate scarcity; contrast free price feedback loops with the structural shortages of socialist price controls and central planning.
2. Industry & Commerce Ch. 7-10
The self-correcting discipline of profits and losses; contrast market accountability with the systemic waste of subsidized state-run industries under socialism.
3. Work & Pay Ch. 11-14
How productivity determines real wages, contrasting voluntary labor value with state-directed wage fixing and centralized quota systems.
4. Time & Risk Ch. 15-18
The role of capital investment, risk-sharing, and how speculation coordinates future resource needs without a state planner.
5. National Economy Ch. 19-21
Real wealth (physical output) vs. monetary tokens; why statist monetary expansion and deficit printing inevitably destroy currencies.
6. International Economy Ch. 22-24
The non-zero-sum nature of voluntary trade and comparative advantage vs. state-directed protectionism.
7. Special Issues Ch. 25-27
Non-market decision-making; contrast political bureaucracy and NGO incentives with the direct accountability of free enterprise.
"The first rule of economics is scarcity; the first rule of politics is to ignore the first rule of economics." — Sowell
